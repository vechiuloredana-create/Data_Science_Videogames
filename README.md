# Data_Science_Videogames
This project explores a video game sales dataset to answer several questions about the industry:

Which publisher has the highest sales per game? <br>
What platforms have the highest global sales? <br>
Which decade produced the best-selling games?<br>
How does genre popularity compare across regions?<br>
What features correlate with high user scores?<br>
How have video games changed over the years?<br>



Which publisher has the highest sales per game?<br>
On average Palcome has the highesr sales per games followed by Red Orb.

What platforms have the highest global sales?<br>
The platform with the highest global sales is PS2, while the lowest is PCFX.

Which decade produced the best-selling games?<br>
Looking at the total sales, more recend decades like 2000s and 2010s have the highest combined sales, but thats maynly because more games where released during those decades compared to the other decades. Also as time went on the median of the games sales keept decreasing which implies that games were less bought as decades went. Also the graph shows no imformation for the 2020 as theres only one entry so the data for that decade can be ignored.

How does genre popularity compare across resiong?<br>
Action seems to be the most popular game genre accros all regions but the graph show that it's really popular in North America with over ~800M sales and ~500M sales in Europe. However Role-Playing games stood out as its the only genre where the sales in Japan surpass the North American ones, making it the most sold genre in Japan.This shows that there isnt one "most popular genre" accros all regions but each region has its own most favorite one. 

What features correlate with high user score?<br>
Critic Score has a correlation of 0.58 with User Score making it the strongest relationship compared to the others.So games the tend to be scored high by Critics tend to also be score high by Users, which is something you would expect.<br>
Year of Release has a correlation of -0.27 with User Score, hence making it a negative correlation between the two. Which implies that older games tend to have more user score than the newer ones.<br>
Also it seems that Global Sales and Critic Score dosent have a stong correlation (0.25) which means people don't tend to look at the critic scores when buying a game. However the correlation between user score and global sales is a weaker correlation (0.091). So poeple look at other factors when buying games not the scores.

How have video games changed over the years?<br>
The line shows a slow steady inncrease between 1980 and 1995, then between 1996 and ~2008 theres an acelleration in growth, and then a peoak between 2008-10 of ~680M Sales.And then after that there was a decline in sales, which may be because a limitation of data in the dataset for those years.
